Introduction

The inspiration for designing a wiretap and dnr dashboard comes from The Wire; a television series on HBO which first aired in 2002. The show illustrates various themes and institutions within the city of Baltimore. With wiretap investigations being the main propeller driving the story forward, The Wire provides fictional, but extremely realistic, character studies with personal motivations, behaviors and pain-points within the War on Drugs, labor unions, politics, education and the media.

The special detail in the series face battles and blowback from various different areas:

  • in-fighting among the detail
  • politically motivated stonewalling from above
  • the ever-evolving malleability of the drug dealers in Baltimore

Goal:

Design a dashboard design to be used by law enforcement and judicial personnel. The dashboard should help in the following areas:

  • assist a team's efforts in organizing the specifics job functions of each member
  • provide wiretaps and wiretap information for discoverable evidence and legal purposes
  • make information accessible to all unit members at any location for adaptability

Possible Contraints

Given the nature of basing the project on a fictional television show, there are a few contraints:

  • The ability to collect qualitative data from the characters portrayed in the show, via interviewing, will not be possible.
  • The characters exist in a universe where the year is approximate 2003

Personas

I chose three characters from the show, representing three important functions, within the investigation. There's a unit commander, detective and prosecutor.

  • Cedric Daniels - Lieutenant and Shift Commander of The Narcotics Unit. Daniels was assigned to lead the Barksdale investigation after Detective McNulty, from the Homicide Unit, swades a judge to order an investigation.

  • Rhonda Pearlman - Rhonda Pearlman is a prosecuting attorney and reliable liaison for Cedric Daniels' investigations. Rhonda has deep knowledge and a wide array of connections within Baltimore's judicial system.

  • Lester Freamon - Detective Freamon, formerly a homicide detetive, was reassigned to the Pawn Shop Unit for stepping on too many toes via being "good police". He was assigned to the Barksdale detail and proved to be an extremely intelligent investigator.
