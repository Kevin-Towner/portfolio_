Introduction

My redesign of NJ Transit's mobile experience has been an iterative process stemming from my capstone project at Brainstation's UX Design course. Since graduating from the course, NJ Transit has redesigned their mobile app and updated the interface.

My redesign is based on qualitative insights from NJ Transit passengers and aimed at improving the Trip Planner tools by adding more functionality without compromising accessibility.

  • Who is using the the NJ Transit app?
  • What are NJ Transit passengers' biggest woes with the rail system
  • Where are the majority of NJ Transit passengers going? (high level)
  • When are NJ Transit rail cars the most busy?
  • Why do people use the NJ Transit app?

Assumptions

  • I believe employed people between the ages of 21 and 45 are the ones mostly using the NJ Transit app.
  • I believe the biggest woes of NJ Transit passengers is the lack of seating on the train.
  • I believe the majority of NJ Transit passengers are going to and from work.
  • I believe the NJ Transit rail is the most busy in the morning
  • I believe people use the NJ Transit app to purchase tickets and stay up to date on schedules.

Takeaway

I believe assumption b (I believe the biggest woes of NJ Transit passengers is the lack of available seating on the train) is relatively risky because there is no correlation between purchased tickets and a type of assigned seating. Attempting to solve an issue revolving around seating availability could end up making NJ Transit's app more complicated than it has to be since there is no assigned seating.

Qualitative Research

I communicated with people on Reddit under the subgroup "r/newjersey" and asked a series of questions to gain insights into the travel habits and attitudes of NJ Transit passengers. The responses can be categorized into motivations, behaviors and pain-points
