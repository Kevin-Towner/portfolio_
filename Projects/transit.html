Introduction

This is my second iteration of the NJ Transit, Trip Planning, redesign. My first rendition of NJ Transit was a part of my capstone project at Brainstation, in New York, NY.

What I hope to discover

  • Who finds NJ Transit's app the most helpful?
  • What other apps are popular for commuter purposes?
  • Where in the process of traveling via public transit do people experience the most stress?
  • When do people decide to use another mobile app, besides NJ Transit, to help them get to where they're going?
  • Why do people prefer public transportation over driving their personal vehicels?
  • How often do people need to use a GPS-based navigation app after they have gotten to their NJ Transit destination?

Qualitative Analysis

Upon conducting qualitative interviews with four NJ Transit passengers, I categorized segments of the interviewees' responses into three main categories:

  • Motivations
  • Behaviors
  • Pain Points

With the responses being organized and the archetypes clearly defined, I am able to develop two user personas based on the needs and experiences of the interviewees.

  • Baby Boomers
  • Millennials

Research Recap

Discovery

  • Who finds NJ Transit's app the most helpful?
  • - Boomers and Millennials

  • What other apps are popular for commuter purposes?
  • - Google Maps and Waze

  • Where in the process of traveling via public transit do people experience the most stress?
  • - After arriving at their NJ Transit Destination (at arrival)

  • When do people decide to use another mobile app, besides NJ Transit, to help them get to where they're going?
  • - Once they arrive and leave the scope of NJ Transit

  • Why do people prefer public transportation over a personal vehicle?
  • - aside from Uber or another ride-sharing app, personal vehicles are not available at desired train stations

  • How often do people need to use a GPS-based navigation app after they have gotten to their NJ Transit destination?
  • - often

Millennials:

  • use Google Maps for foot/walking directions
  • prefer Waze over Google Maps for driving directions and real-time traffic patterns
  • use NJ Transit's app for checking schedules and buying tickets
  • would prefer not to use Uber or Uber competitor app due to the financial cost and excessibility of the NYC Metro

Baby Boomers:

  • use Google Maps for foot/walking & driving direction
  • prefer Google Maps over Waze for its reliability and the fact that Waze is owned by Google
  • use NJ Transit's app for checking schedules and buying tickets
  • would prefer to use Uber for convenience

Information Architecture

Prototyping

Homescreen and Trip Planning Accessibility

Planning a trip is available from the 'recents' tab and from the center icon on the bottom nav

Departure, Arrival and Travel Date

When searching departing and arriving addresses, filtering is done without a dropdown box from the input form

Travel Time and Route Ticketing

Users can purchase tickets within each route itinerary once the date and time of trip is selected
